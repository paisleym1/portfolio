Part 1: Merging Meshes

First thing I did was go to Thingiverse and download two meshes, this

PacMan Ghost

and this

Top Hat

. I wanted to merge the Top Hat with the PacMan Ghost so that the ghost would be wearing the top hat, so I exported the two files to Rhino. I reduced the polygon count of the meshes to make everything easier for me to work with.

Merging the two meshes proved much harder than I thought it would. The top hat is a closed mesh with a hollow space in the middle, so when I merged it with the ghost it still had that empty space inside it, as well as overlapping parts. Basically, it was a bad mesh. I could not figure out how to fill in the top hat, so I opted to make my own since it's only two shapes and really not that complicated.

I was able to create my own top hat easily by making two solids, turning them into meshes, and then using MeshBooleanUnion. I also sized it so it was look good on the ghost's head.
